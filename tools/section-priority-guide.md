## Quick Start Path

**Time**: 2-4 hours
**Goal**: Minimum viable context to use with AI tools today

### Fill These 5 Sections (in order):

#### 1. Executive Summary (30 min)
**Why first**: Gives AI basic understanding of who you are

**Fill**:
- Company name and what you do
- Key numbers (revenue, customers, employees)
- Main differentiator (1 sentence)

**Skip for now**:
- Detailed history
- Long mission statements

---

#### 2. Products and Services (1 hour)
**Why second**: AI needs to know what you're building

**Fill**:
- Main product/service name
- What it does (1-2 paragraphs)
- Who uses it
- Key features (bullet list)

**Skip for now**:
- Competitive analysis
- Detailed pricing
- Minor products

---

#### 3. Technology Stack (1 hour)
**Why third**: Critical for AI to make tech decisions

**Fill**:
```yaml
technology_landscape:
  core_technologies:
    frontend: "[Your frontend tech]"
    backend: "[Your backend tech]"
    database: "[Your database]"
    infrastructure: "[Cloud provider or on-premise]"

  architecture_style: "[Microservices/Monolithic/Hybrid]"
```

**Skip for now**:
- Detailed architecture diagrams
- Integration details
- Technical debt explanation

---

#### 4. Main Constraints (30 min)
**Why fourth**: AI must respect these boundaries

**Fill**:
- Regulatory requirements (e.g., "GDPR compliant - affects user data handling")
- Key security requirements (e.g., "All data encrypted at rest and in transit")
- Main technology constraint (e.g., "Must use AWS - existing enterprise agreement")

**Skip for now**:
- Detailed compliance matrices
- All edge cases

---

#### 5. Strategic Direction (30 min)
**Why fifth**: Helps AI align with where you're going

**Fill**:
- Main goal for next 12 months
- 2-3 key initiatives
- What success looks like

**Skip for now**:
- Detailed roadmaps
- Long-term vision

## Startup Path

**Time**: 4-8 hours
**Best for**: Startups, small teams (< 50 people), single product

### Priority Order:

**Phase 1: Foundation (2-3 hours)**
1. Executive Summary (30 min)
2. Core Business & Value Proposition (1 hour)
3. Products and Services (1 hour)
4. Technology Landscape (30-60 min)

**Phase 2: Critical Context (2-3 hours)**
5. Customer Segments (1 hour) - Focus on 1-2 main segments
6. Constraints and Requirements (1 hour)
7. Strategic Objectives (30-60 min)

**Phase 3: Nice to Have (2-3 hours)**
8. Competitive Landscape (1 hour)
9. Success Metrics (30-60 min)
10. Company Culture (30 min)

### Startup-Specific Tips:

**Do**:
- Be honest about early stage ("Pre-revenue", "10 customers")
- Focus on vision and direction
- Document what makes you different
- Include founder insights

**Skip**:
- Complex capability models
- Detailed process documentation
- Extensive partnership lists
- Long business history

---

## Scale-up Path

**Time**: 8-16 hours
**Best for**: Growing companies (50-500 people), multiple products

### Priority Order:

**Phase 1: Core (4-6 hours)**
1. Executive Summary (1 hour) - More detailed than startup
2. Company Profile (1 hour) - Org structure matters now
3. Core Business & Value Proposition (1-2 hours)
4. Products and Services (2 hours) - Multiple products to document
5. Technology Landscape (2 hours) - More complex now

**Phase 2: Strategic (3-5 hours)**
6. Customer Segments (2 hours) - Multiple segments
7. Strategic Objectives (1-2 hours)
8. Business Capabilities (2 hours) - Starting to matter
9. Constraints and Requirements (1-2 hours)

**Phase 3: Operational (3-5 hours)**
10. Competitive Landscape (1-2 hours)
11. Operating Model (1 hour)
12. Success Metrics (1 hour)
13. Partnerships (1 hour)
14. Risks and Challenges (1 hour)

### Scale-up-Specific Tips:

**Do**:
- Document how things used to work vs. how they work now
- Capture lessons learned from early mistakes
- Explain why you're scaling things
- Note organizational growing pains

**Focus on**:
- Scaling challenges
- Process maturity
- Team growth
- Technical debt

## Enterprise Path

**Time**: 16-40 hours
**Best for**: Large organizations (500+ people), complex structures

### Priority Order:

**Phase 1: Foundation (4-6 hours)**
1. Executive Summary (2 hours) - Comprehensive
2. Company Profile (2 hours) - Complex org structure
3. Business History (2 hours) - Context matters

**Phase 2: Business Architecture (6-10 hours)**
4. Core Business & Value Proposition (2-3 hours)
5. Products and Services (3-4 hours) - Large portfolio
6. Customer Segments (2-3 hours) - Multiple segments
7. Business Capabilities (3-4 hours) - TOGAF model

**Phase 3: Governance & Strategy (4-8 hours)**
8. Operating Model (2-3 hours)
9. Strategic Objectives (2-3 hours)
10. Stakeholders (2 hours)
11. Constraints and Requirements (2-3 hours)

**Phase 4: Operations (6-10 hours)**
12. Technology Landscape (3-4 hours) - Complex
13. Business Processes (2-3 hours)
14. Data and Analytics (2 hours)
15. Partnerships (2 hours)

**Phase 5: Context & Culture (4-8 hours)**
16. Competitive Landscape (2 hours)
17. Success Metrics (2 hours)
18. Risks and Challenges (2 hours)
19. Organizational Culture (2 hours)
20. All remaining sections (varies)

### Enterprise-Specific Tips:

**Do**:
- Get cross-functional input
- Document governance clearly
- Explain decision rights
- Note compliance requirements carefully

**Consider**:
- Creating BU-specific versions
- Documenting architecture principles
- Detailed capability models
- Formal governance frameworks

## Problem-Specific Paths

### Problem: "AI Suggests Wrong Technology"

**Fill these sections:**
1. Technology Landscape (Section 10) - Detail current stack
2. Constraints and Requirements (Section 15) - Why certain tech required
3. Lessons Learned (Section 21) - Past tech mistakes

**Time**: 2-3 hours

---

### Problem: "AI Doesn't Respect Our Security/Compliance"

**Fill these sections:**
1. Constraints and Requirements (Section 15) - Detailed compliance
2. Risks and Challenges (Section 18) - Security risks
3. Technology Landscape (Section 10) - Security architecture

**Time**: 2-3 hours

---

### Problem: "AI Doesn't Understand Our Customers"

**Fill these sections:**
1. Customer Segments (Section 6) - Detailed profiles
2. Core Business & Value Proposition (Section 4) - Why they choose you
3. Products and Services (Section 5) - Customer segments per product

**Time**: 2-4 hours

---

### Problem: "AI Suggestions Don't Align with Strategy"

**Fill these sections:**
1. Strategic Objectives (Section 13) - Clear goals
2. Future Vision (Section 22) - Where you're heading
3. Business Capabilities (Section 8) - What needs to improve

**Time**: 2-4 hours

---

### Problem: "New Developers Don't Get Context"

**Fill these sections:**
1. Executive Summary (Section 1) - Quick overview
2. Company Profile (Section 2) - Org structure
3. Business History (Section 3) - How we got here
4. Organizational Culture (Section 19) - How we work
5. Technology Landscape (Section 10) - Tech environment

**Time**: 3-5 hours
